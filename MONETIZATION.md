# Monetization Notes

Maker: `city in the sky`

## Product Positioning

Name:

```text
SaaS Competitor Battlecard Generator
```

Short pitch:

```text
Turn public SaaS websites into sales-ready competitor battlecards, pricing signals, differentiation angles, and objection handlers.
```

Primary buyer:

- SaaS founders;
- product marketers;
- sales teams;
- agencies selling positioning, CRO, SEO, or outbound services;
- consultants who need fast public competitive intelligence.

## Pricing

Apify Store:

- start at USD 0.19-0.29 per successful competitor battlecard;
- raise toward USD 0.49 if model-backed reports are consistently useful;
- charge only after one competitor battlecard succeeds;
- set a max charge cap when publishing so buyers know the run ceiling.

Recommended Apify event:

```text
competitor-battlecard-generated
```

Manual starter offer:

- USD 19 for 5 competitor battlecards;
- USD 49 for 15 competitor battlecards;
- USD 99-249 for a full competitor pack plus rewritten positioning and sales copy.

Crypto-first order terms:

- USDT or USDC preferred;
- PayPal available as backup;
- buyer provides only public company and competitor URLs.

## Distribution

Low-friction first channels:

- Apify Actor Store listing;
- GitHub README with sample output;
- city in the sky service site as a manual-order landing page;
- X demo post aimed at SaaS founders and product marketers;
- direct replies to founder/agency posts asking about competitors, positioning, pricing, or landing page copy.

## Why This Is Better Than Raw API Resale

- Customers pay for a finished competitive intelligence workflow, not tokens.
- The output can be used directly in sales calls, landing page reviews, and product marketing notes.
- Costs can be capped by page, character, and competitor limits.
- One useful report can justify a higher price than raw scraped rows.

## What Not To Accept

- private account pages, customer data, credentials, or scraped personal contacts;
- claims that require non-public data;
- guaranteed sales, investment, legal, medical, or compliance outcomes;
- spam campaign sending;
- KYC, banking, wallet, rebate, captcha, account, or verification-code work;
- unclear jobs that require hidden data or identity abuse.
